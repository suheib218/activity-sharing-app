Activity Manager Documentation

Value Proposition

Problem

People often face difficulty organizing spontaneous activities with friends due to poor communication, lack of a centralized planning tool, and time constraints. Social platforms are not specifically designed for managing events, and the constant back-and-forth makes it difficult to quickly plan and execute activities. This leads to missed opportunities for social engagement and frustration among users.

Solution

The Activity Sharing App provides an easy-to-use platform for creating, sharing, and managing events in real time. With features that allow users to create activities with a few clicks, join events, and coordinate effortlessly with friends, the app eliminates the stress of planning spontaneous activities. Whether it's a quick coffee meeting or a weekend outing, users can stay connected and ensure their plans come together smoothly.

Target group

My target group includes socially active individuals, particularly young professionals, students, and groups of friends looking for quick and easy ways to plan events. Additionally, the app appeals to small communities or clubs that need a tool for managing and sharing activities without the complexity of formal scheduling apps. The app is also useful for anyone who values efficiency in organizing their social life.

Customer Journey

1. Starting Point: Registration & Profile Creation

User Sign-Up & Login:

  • Step 1: New users arrive at the platform's homepage. They are prompted to either register or log in to their account.
  • Step 2: For new users, the registration process involves filling out basic details such as username, email, and password. Returning users simply log in using their credentials.
  • Step 3: After successful registration or login, users are directed home page.
2. Exploration of Activities and Other Users' Profiles

Browse and Discover:

  • Step 1: After setting up their profile, users are taken to the activity exploration page. Here, they can browse a wide variety of activities organized by category (e.g., sports, workshops, networking events).
  • Step 2: Users can view detailed information about each activity such as the event title, description, date and time, location (if applicable), and the number of participants.
  • Step 3: To help make decisions, users can view profiles of the activity organizers and other participants.
3. Selection and Participation: Joining Activities

Join Activities:

  • Step 1: After exploring activities, users select an activity to participate in.
  • Step 2: Users review the activity’s detailed page, which includes a full description.
  • Step 3: To confirm participation, users click on a "Join" button.
3. Selection and Participation: Joining Activities

Join Activities:

  • Step 1: After exploring activities, users select an activity to participate in.
  • Step 2: Users review the activity’s detailed page, which includes a full description.
  • Step 3: To confirm participation, users click on a "Join" button.

Manage Joined Activities:

  • Step 1: Users can manage their participation from the "My Activities" section.
  • Step 2: Users can see which activities they have created, joined and view upcoming events.
4. Activity Management and Interaction:

Users: Manage their own activities by editing details or removing them if necessary.
